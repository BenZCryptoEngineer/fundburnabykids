# PAC Outreach Templates

**Trigger:** any time you want to recruit a SD41 PAC to formally endorse the campaign. There is no single "milestone" for this — PAC outreach runs continuously through the campaign, fastest in the 2–3 weeks before the May 27 budget adoption.
**Owner:** Ben Zhou + any signed-up parent willing to nudge their own kid's PAC.

The PAC kit content (motion text, two-minute briefing, Q&A, sources) already lives at https://fundburnabykids.ca/pac-kit/ and inside the PAC modal on the homepage. **This document is the email scaffolding that gets the PAC kit in front of the PAC chair in the first place.**

Five templates below, each with the exact subject line and body, in copy-paste-ready form. Pick the right one for the situation.

---

## Variables to fill before any template goes out

| Variable | Where it comes from | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `{PAC_NAME}` | the official PAC name | `Capitol Hill Elementary PAC` |
| `{SCHOOL_NAME}` | school name (without "PAC") | `Capitol Hill Elementary` |
| `{CHAIR_NAME}` | PAC chair's name (from PAC's website / school directory) | `Sarah Lin` |
| `{CHAIR_FIRST}` | chair's first name only | `Sarah` |
| `{YOUR_NAME}` | the sender's full name | `Ben Zhou` / `Maria Tang` |
| `{YOUR_FIRST}` | sender's first name | `Ben` / `Maria` |
| `{YOUR_RELATION}` | sender's connection to the school (variant B only) | `parent of two daughters at the school` |
| `{NEXT_PAC_DATE}` | the next PAC meeting date if known (variant A optional) | `May 14` |
| `{N}` | total confirmed signatures on fundburnabykids.ca that day | `523` |

## Variant A — Ben direct (cold or semi-cold)

**Use when:** Ben is reaching out to a PAC chair he has not previously talked to. Higher friction; needs more "who I am, why I'm writing." Keep it short.

**Subject:** `5-minute ask: PAC endorsement for $9.4M SD41 funding before May 27`

```
Dear {CHAIR_FIRST},

I'm Ben Zhou, a Burnaby parent of two daughters at SD41 schools and
the founder of a parent-led campaign called Fund Burnaby Kids.
We're asking the Province to fully fund the $9.4 million arbitration
liability facing SD41 from the $5 billion Contingencies Vote in
Budget 2026 — before SD41 adopts its 2026-27 budget on May 27.

{N} Burnaby parents have written so far. The case, the source
documents, and the public list are at https://fundburnabykids.ca.

I'd like to ask {PAC_NAME} to formally endorse the campaign. What
that does: your school appears publicly on fundburnabykids.ca, the
endorsement is counted in our delivery to the Minister, and you
share the campaign with your school families through your existing
channels. What it doesn't do: any money, any party affiliation,
any liability.

I've prepared a 2-page kit specifically for PAC meetings — motion
text, a two-minute briefing your members can read aloud, and the
top questions members typically ask:

  https://fundburnabykids.ca/pac-kit/

If your PAC decides to endorse, the chair can submit on the same
page (the form takes about 60 seconds and we verify by email
within 24 hours).

The motion is short enough to add to a meeting agenda this month.
If your next PAC meeting is before May 27, that's the ideal venue.

Happy to brief you or your executive directly by phone or in
person — I am available evenings and weekends through May.

Thank you for considering this.

Sincerely,

Ben Zhou
Burnaby parent · founder, Burnaby Kids First
ben@fundburnabykids.ca
https://fundburnabykids.ca
```

## Variant B — Parent forward (warm, highest conversion)

**Use when:** a Burnaby parent who has already signed the petition wants to nudge their own kid's PAC. This is the strongest channel because PAC chairs trust their own community. Ben can send this template to signers and ask them to forward.

**Subject:** `Could {PAC_NAME} endorse this? — about the $9.4M SD41 funding ask`

```
Hi {CHAIR_FIRST},

{YOUR_RELATION} writing personally to flag a parent-led campaign
I think {PAC_NAME} should consider endorsing.

The campaign is called Fund Burnaby Kids
(https://fundburnabykids.ca). The ask is precise: the Province
should fund the $9.4 million arbitration liability facing SD41 from
the $5 billion Contingencies Vote that Budget 2026 already sets
aside for exactly this kind of cost. The deadline matters — SD41
adopts its 2026-27 budget on May 27, after which any cuts become
structural.

I signed last week. {N} Burnaby parents have written so far.

I know PAC time is precious. The campaign organizer (Ben Zhou)
prepared a 2-page kit specifically for PAC meetings — motion text,
two-minute briefing, the questions members usually ask:

  https://fundburnabykids.ca/pac-kit/

Three things that helped me decide it was worth bringing to you:

  1. It is not partisan. The ask works under any government.
  2. There is no money or liability from the PAC.
  3. The campaign is verifiable — every signer is email-confirmed
     and the source documents are linked on the site.

If {PAC_NAME} decides to put it on the next agenda, the motion
text on the kit page is ready to copy.

Happy to attend the meeting if helpful. Either way, thanks for
considering it.

{YOUR_NAME}
{YOUR_RELATION}
```

## Variant C — Follow-up (no response after ~10 days)

**Use when:** Variant A or B was sent and there's been no reply for ~10 calendar days. Stay light; don't pressure.

**Subject:** `Re: 5-minute ask: PAC endorsement for $9.4M SD41 funding`  *(reply on the original thread — keep the same subject so it threads)*

```
Hi {CHAIR_FIRST},

Just bumping this in case it slipped past your inbox.

The May 27 budget date is now {DAYS_OUT} days away, and we've
crossed {N} confirmed signatures from Burnaby parents.

If {PAC_NAME} would rather not take a position on this, that's
completely fine — please just reply with a short "no" and I'll
take the school off our outreach list. No follow-up needed.

If a 10-minute call would help your executive decide, I'm
available {2_OR_3_DAYS_THIS_WEEK}.

Thanks,

{YOUR_FIRST}
```

## Variant D — Graceful decline acknowledgment

**Use when:** a PAC chair replies "no thanks" or "the PAC decided not to take a position." This template costs almost nothing and keeps the relationship intact for any future Burnaby Kids First campaign.

**Subject:** *(reply on their thread; don't change subject)*

```
Hi {CHAIR_FIRST},

Thank you for getting back to me, and for taking the time to
consider it. Completely understood.

I'll take {PAC_NAME} off the outreach list for this campaign. If
the situation changes — or if a future Burnaby Kids First campaign
is closer to your PAC's priorities — I'll keep your contact
respectfully.

Best wishes for the rest of the school year.

{YOUR_FIRST}
```

Sincerely,

Ben Zhou
ben@fundburnabykids.ca

## Tips for parents using Variant B

If you're a Burnaby parent forwarding the campaign to your own kid's PAC, three notes that significantly raise the response rate:

1. **Personalize the opening sentence.** Replace `{YOUR_RELATION}` with something specific: *"Sara Tang here — Mei's mom in Mr. Chen's Grade 4"*. PAC chairs read more carefully when they recognize the sender's kid.
2. **Attach nothing.** Inline links only. PAC chairs don't open PDFs from new senders. The campaign site loads in 1 second on mobile.
3. **Send from a personal Gmail / Outlook, not a work address.** PAC chairs trust personal-domain senders. Don't BCC anyone — looks like spam.
4. **Don't follow up more than once.** If they don't reply, drop it. The strongest move you can make at that point is to publicly sign the petition — it shows up on the school's tally automatically.

Worth doing because (a) prevents duplicate sends from Ben + a parent both reaching out on the same day, (b) the data point — "what % of PACs that received Variant A endorsed" — is feedback for the play.

## Order-of-operations if you have a list of 30 PACs to reach

1. **Day 1:** Variant A to PACs where Ben has direct contact (DPAC connections, prior school events, friends-of-friends).
2. **Day 2–3:** Email signers who chose `pending_consent_updates=true`, ask if they're willing to forward Variant B to their own kid's PAC. Pre-fill the template with their kid's school in the subject.
3. **Day 7 onward:** start follow-ups (Variant C) for any PAC from Day 1 that hasn't responded.
4. **Day 14:** anyone who declined gets Variant D, kept warm for next year.
5. **Endorsement comes in:** Variant E within 24h, every time.

Don't try to hit 30 PACs in one day. Each pitch deserves the chair's name and a glance at the school site to confirm context. 5–8 per day is the realistic upper bound; more than that and the personalization slips.
