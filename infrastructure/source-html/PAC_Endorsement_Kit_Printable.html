The situation · in full

A provincial salary arbitration ordered Burnaby School District 41 to pay 800+ teachers retroactive wages for a grid-classification error. The compounded cost to SD41 for 2026-27 is approximately $9.4 million. This liability is not in the Province's funding formula.

The single most important fact: BCPSEA — the BC Public School Employers' Association, which is the Province's own bargaining agent — has publicly acknowledged the cost would have been funded in 2022 had they interpreted the salary grid correctly. This is not a dispute about whether the money is owed. It is a dispute about who pays for the Province's own error: the Province, or Burnaby students.

The ask: Fund the $9.4M from BC Budget 2026's Contingencies Vote ($5B/year), which the Treasury Board itself describes as covering "collective bargaining mandate costs." $9.4M is 0.19% of that fund — not new money, existing money applied to its stated purpose.

What endorsing means — and doesn't mean

Your PAC does:
  • Appear publicly on fundburnabykids.ca
  • Share with your school families
  • Be counted when we deliver to the Minister
Your PAC does NOT:
  • Give up independence or authority
  • Contribute money or resources
  • Endorse any political party or candidate
  • Take on any legal liability

Proposed motion

Standard motion — verbatim "That the [School Name] PAC endorse the Fund Burnaby Kids parent coalition calling on the Province of British Columbia to fully fund the $9.4 million arbitration liability affecting Burnaby School District 41 from the Budget 2026 Contingencies Vote, and that the PAC chair be authorized to submit this endorsement on behalf of the PAC."

Questions PAC members are likely to ask

Q: Is this partisan?

No. The campaign names zero political parties and zero candidates. The ask is a technical budget request any government could action. MLAs are listed because they represent Burnaby ridings, regardless of party.

Q: Is DPAC behind this?

Burnaby DPAC has publicly advocated the same position. Fund Burnaby Kids is independent but aligned, operating on a faster timeline than DPAC's monthly cycle allows.

Q: Who runs this?

Ben Zhou — Burnaby parent, founder of Expeta Technologies. Fully public identity. No political career, no party affiliation. Architecture designed to outlive any single organizer.

Q: What if the Province funds it — was endorsing worth it?

Yes. If funding happens, the coalition moved the decision. If funding doesn't, the coalition becomes the foundation for next year. Either way, endorsing has value.
