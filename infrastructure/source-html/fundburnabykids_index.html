Three things the Province already knows
省府其实早就知道的三件事

This is not a budget problem. It is a decision problem.

这不是预算问题，而是一个决策问题。

01

BCPSEA admits this is their interpretive error.

BCPSEA承认这是他们的解读错误。

The BC Public School Employers' Association bargains on behalf of every BC school district. An arbitrator ruled that beginning teachers in Burnaby were placed one step too low on the salary grid as of July 1, 2022. SD41 was not a party to that negotiation. Board Chair Kristin Schnider has stated publicly that the costs would have been fully funded at the time if BCPSEA had interpreted the agreement correctly. The cost landed on Burnaby — and only Burnaby — because of a provincial-level interpretive failure.

BCPSEA（BC省公立学校雇主协会）代表BC每一个学区谈判。仲裁员裁定，自2022年7月1日起，Burnaby的初任教师在薪资等级表上被低估了一级。SD41并非谈判方。董事会主席Kristin Schnider公开表示：如果BCPSEA当时正确解读协议，这笔钱本就会被全额资助。这笔成本落到Burnaby——只落到Burnaby——是因为省级层面的解读失误。

02

Burnaby has no cushion left.

Burnaby已经没有缓冲空间了。

In 2025-26, SD41 cut $4.2 million — high school counsellors, custodial staff, the Grade 7 band program serving 1,200 students, advanced learning, and Mandarin. Unrestricted reserves sit at $4.3 million. The arbitration liability is more than double that. "Doing more with less" has already been done. What comes next is cutting from the bone.

2025-26年，SD41已削减$4.2M——高中辅导员、清洁人员、服务1,200名学生的7年级乐队项目、advanced learning课程、中文课。非限制性储备金只有$4.3M。这次的仲裁责任是它的两倍多。"用更少的钱做更多的事"已经做到头了。接下来只能砍到骨头。

03

Budget 2026 reserved $5 billion a year for exactly this.

2026预算每年留了$5B就是为了这种情况。

The Contingencies Vote in Budget 2026 is explicitly described as covering "caseload pressures, current collective bargaining mandate costs, and other costs that are uncertain at the time of building the budget." The $9.4M Burnaby liability is almost verbatim a collective bargaining mandate cost. It represents 0.19% of the annual Contingencies Vote. The money exists. What remains is the decision to apply it.

2026预算中的应急基金明确说明用于"案件压力、当前集体谈判授权成本、以及其他在编制预算时不确定的成本"。Burnaby的$9.4M几乎逐字匹配"集体谈判授权成本"的定义。这笔钱占应急基金年度拨款的0.19%。钱已经在那里，剩下的只是决定是否使用。

    Will sending an email really change anything? 发一封邮件真的能改变什么吗？

    Ministerial Correspondence Units sort incoming mail by postal code. When Burnaby MLAs receive dozens of identical-subject emails in the same week from their own constituents, that becomes a political signal that reaches caucus. The Province has already confirmed they are "actively engaged" on this file — that means the decision is open.

    The petition serves a different purpose: it creates a named collective object that can be printed, counted, and physically delivered. Emails are volume; petitions are weight. We need both.

    省府厅长通信单位按邮编分类处理邮件。当Burnaby的MLA在同一周内收到数十封来自本选区居民的相同主题邮件，这就成为一个会传达到党团的政治信号。省府已确认他们正在"积极处理"此事——这意味着决定尚未做出，仍在讨论中。

    请愿有不同作用：它形成一个可打印、可计数、可亲手递交的有名有姓的集体文件。邮件是数量，请愿是重量。两者都需要。

    Yes. Uncheck the third box in the consent section before submitting. Your name will still appear on the petition. We recommend doing both — they reach different parts of government — but signing alone is fully valid.

    What about CASL — do your emails comply? CASL呢——你们的邮件合规吗？

    CASL covers "commercial electronic messages." Political advocacy emails fall outside that definition (see Industry Canada guidance and leading non-profit legal analysis). Even so, we follow CASL best practice: we obtain express opt-in consent for updates, every email has a one-click unsubscribe, every email identifies us with a physical address, and we honour unsubscribes within 10 days — well under the statutory requirement.

    CASL仅监管"商业电子信息"。政治倡导类邮件不属于该定义（见加拿大产业部指引及主要非营利组织的法律分析）。即便如此，我们仍遵循CASL最佳实践：明确获取订阅同意、每封邮件含一键退订、每封邮件包含实体地址、退订请求在10天内处理——远低于法定要求。

    Who sees my email after I sign? 我签名后谁会看到我的邮件？

    When you click "Open my pre-filled email," your email client (Apple Mail, Gmail, Outlook) opens with the recipients, subject, and body already filled in. You review and click send — from your own account. We never see the email leave your machine, and we never receive a copy. The Ministry and MLAs see the letter came from your personal address, which is what makes it a constituent letter.

    点击"打开我的预填邮件"时，您的邮件客户端（Apple Mail、Gmail、Outlook）会打开，收件人、主题和正文都已预填好。您审阅后点击发送——从您自己的账户。我们看不到邮件离开您的设备，也不会收到副本。省府和MLA看到的是来自您个人地址的邮件，这正是它成为"选民邮件"的关键。

    Answers to questions your PAC members might ask

    PAC成员可能提出的问题的回答

    • "Is this partisan?" — No. The campaign does not name any political party or candidate. The ask (apply the Contingencies Vote) is a technical budget request that any government of any party could do.
    • "这是党派行为吗？" — 不是。活动不提任何政党或候选人。诉求（应用应急基金）是一个技术性预算请求，任何党派的政府都可以做。
    • "Is DPAC behind this?" — DPAC has publicly advocated for the same position. This coalition is parent-led and aligned with DPAC's stance, but organizationally independent so it can move faster than DPAC's monthly cycle allows.
    • "DPAC在后面吗？" — DPAC已公开支持同一立场。此联盟由家长主导，与DPAC立场一致，但组织上独立，能比DPAC的月度周期更快行动。
    • "What if we endorse and the Province funds it — was it worth it?" — Yes. If funding happens, the coalition and the signatures were evidence that moved the needle. If funding doesn't happen, the list becomes the foundation for next year's harder campaign.
    • "如果我们背书后省府拨款了——值得吗？" — 值得。如果资金到位，联盟和签名就是推动了改变的证据。如果资金未到位，这份名单将成为来年更艰难活动的基础。
